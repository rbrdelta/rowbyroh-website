01Case Study

The Obsidian MCP server started as a way to give Claude direct access to my personal knowledge base. Four phases in, it has become the connective tissue between every tool I use: 672 notes imported from Claude conversations, Apple Notes, and Notion, each classified across a 14-dimension taxonomy that emerged from the data itself rather than being imposed upfront.

What makes it work is the pipeline's idempotence. Every import operation can be re-run safely. Every classification pass refines without destroying. The vault isn't a static archive; it's a living graph where 2,599 auto-generated wikilinks surface connections I never manually drew. The system rewards consistency over cleverness.

"The constraint is the brief."
